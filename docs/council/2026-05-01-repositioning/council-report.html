Norric / Council Memo No. 14

The Kreditvakt
Pressure Test

Malmö · 01 May 2026
Five advisors, one chairman — on the question of scoring, pricing, and sequencing.
Scenario

Investors endorsed Kreditvakt as the wedge. They want simpler scoring (0–20), six-tier pricing with a free entry, the 5 kr search killed, and Enterprise pricing taken off the website. The question is not whether to do this — it's how to do it without amputating the moat.

Council convened to stress-test the proposed structure before code or copy is written.

The Five Advisors
01
Lena Holmberg
Credit Risk Underwriter · 22 yrs Nordea, SEB
Endorse with edits

The 0–20 scale is correct, but only if the bands map to action, not feeling. "Bevaka" and "Förhöjd risk" are vague — credit committees want decision triggers. Rename them. A 13–16 score should read "Stoppa nya krediter" or "Kräv säkerhet". That's how a real risk officer reads a report at 08:30 on a Monday.

Second concern: a five-band scale collapses too much information for portfolio buyers. Norion Bank monitoring 4,000 SME exposures needs more granularity than a retail Klarna user. Solution: show 0–20 by default, but expose the underlying 0–100 in API responses and Premium dashboards. The investor feedback was about the marketing surface, not the data model.

"A score is a verb disguised as a noun. Make sure yours points to the verb."

Verdict · Ship the 0–20 band, but rewrite the labels as decisions, not adjectives. Keep 0–100 visible in API and Guld+.
Peer review (anonymous) She's right about the labels — "Bevaka" is what a Bolagsverket form says, not what a credit officer needs. But pushing 0–100 visibility into Guld muddies the simplification thesis. Pick one surface or you're back to where you started.
02
Magnus Ek
SaaS Pricing Strategist · ex-Klarna, ex-Tink
Reject the ladder

Six tiers is two tiers too many. Every B2B pricing study from the last decade says the same thing: three to four tiers maximize conversion, six tiers cause analysis paralysis and the customer defaults to the cheapest. You'll cannibalize Silver into Brons and Premium into Guld.

The Free tier is also dangerous in this category. Credit data is a "buy once you trust it" product — if Free shows enough to be useful, no one upgrades. If Free shows nothing, it's a marketing liability. The right wedge is a 14-day full-access trial, not a Free tier. That's how credit bureaus do it. That's how UC and Bisnode do it.

On the 5 kr kill — agreed, but for a different reason than the founder said. 5 kr per search isn't a "hobby tool" signal; it's a willingness-to-pay anchor that destroys all higher prices once it exists. Once a buyer sees 5 kr, 499 kr feels like a 100x markup, not a different product.

"Six tiers is what founders build when they can't decide who their customer is."

Verdict · Compress to four tiers: Trial (14d), Silver (499), Guld (1,499), Enterprise. Drop Brons. Drop Premium as a separate tier — fold into Guld. Drop perpetual Free.
Peer review (anonymous) He's mostly right but overcorrecting. Free does work for credit products if it's lookup-capped and band-only — that's the Creditsafe model and it converts. The real risk isn't tier count, it's tier differentiation. If you can articulate why each tier exists in one sentence, six is fine.
03
Astrid Vinter
GTM Operator · scaled 3 Nordic B2B SaaS to €10M ARR
Endorse, with sequencing concerns

The repositioning is right; the timing is the question. The investor said "use Kreditvakt to launch the rest" — but Kreditvakt has zero paying customers right now. You're being asked to build the product architecture for a portfolio that hasn't been validated by a single contract.

Norion and Serafim outreach is sent but not closed. The right move is to close one paid pilot first — even at a discounted 50,000 SEK annual — and let that customer dictate the pricing tiers. The investor feedback is hypothesis, not data. A signed Norion contract would tell you whether 1,499 kr/month is right or if Guld should be 9,999 kr/month for portfolio monitoring.

Holding the granular debt breakdown behind funding is fine, but make sure the demo to Norion next week shows it as a working feature, not a roadmap promise. Investors funded a vision; buyers fund a product.

Verdict · Don't ship six tiers publicly until one paid pilot validates the band structure. Ship a private price sheet now, public pricing page after first close.
Peer review (anonymous) Strong point on letting the first contract set the price. But "wait for validation" is also how products die in the market. The pricing page is also a sales tool — it sets the anchor when you're negotiating with Norion. Publishing it is part of the close, not a consequence of it.
04
Yuki Tanaka
Product Architect · ex-Stripe, ex-Plaid
Concerns on score model

Translating 0–100 to 0–20 sounds like UX simplification but is actually statistical lossy compression. You're collapsing 5 internal points into 1 displayed point. That's fine for marketing — but you must be careful about three things.

One: boundary effects. A company at internal score 80 (display 16) and one at internal 81 (display 17) jump bands on a one-point change. Customers will see this as instability. Fix: hysteresis — require a 3-point internal change before band moves up or down.

Two: regulatory exposure. If Kreditvakt ends up in MiFID II / consumer credit decisioning territory, displaying simplified bands while making decisions on hidden 0–100 is a black-box AI red flag under the EU AI Act. Compliance tier (norric_explain_score_v1) becomes mandatory, not optional. Worth pricing accordingly.

Three: the granular debt breakdown is the moat, but it's also the GDPR landmine. Listing "unpaid bills" by creditor name pulls in personal data exposure if any creditor is a sole proprietor (enskild firma). Architecturally — strip personal-data fields from the line-item view, show only legal entity creditors in Silver, and put full creditor identity behind Guld with a click-through DPA.

"The score is the marketing. The breakdown is the product. The DPA is the moat."

Verdict · Endorse 0–20 with hysteresis. Reposition norric_explain_score_v1 from "Compliance tier upsell" to "regulatory hedge." Architect debt breakdown around enskild firma exclusion.
Peer review (anonymous) The hysteresis point is excellent and most founders miss it. The EU AI Act framing might be premature — Kreditvakt is B2B credit signal, not consumer-facing decisioning, so Annex III high-risk classification isn't automatic. But preparing for it costs nothing and future-proofs against regulatory drift.
05
Henrik Sjögren
Indie Founder · 3 exits, all bootstrapped
Reject the framing

The investors gave good feedback on a product they don't sell. The 0–20 scoring change is right. The 5 kr removal is right. But "use Kreditvakt to launch the rest" is investor-vocabulary for reduce surface area, and that's the most important thing in this memo.

You have eight active products, one founder, and one closed contract pending. The pricing tier question is a distraction. The real question the investors are asking — politely — is: which products do you stop building?

If Kreditvakt is the wedge, then SIGNAL, Vigil, Sigvik, SiteLoop, Skolradar, and Jarvis are not getting your attention this quarter. That's not a pricing decision; it's a roadmap decision. Make it explicit. Tell the investors: "Q2 is Kreditvakt close, Kreditvakt onboarding, Kreditvakt pricing iteration. Everything else is on maintenance until Q3."

On the pricing tiers themselves: ship whatever you want. The first three customers will tell you what's wrong with it within 90 days, and you'll change it anyway. Don't optimize the page; optimize the close.

"Pricing pages don't sell products. Pipelines do."

Verdict · Pricing decisions are second-order. The first-order decision is which 6 of your 8 products go on ice. Make that explicit before drafting any tier copy.
Peer review (anonymous) The roadmap point is the most important thing in this entire memo and everyone else is dancing around it. But he's wrong that pricing is second-order — for a B2B credit product, the price is the positioning. The 5 kr line communicated "toy"; 1,499 kr communicates "infrastructure." Get it right once, then close.
Chairman's Synthesis

The investors are right about the surface. The council is right about the substance. Both can ship.

The five advisors converge on three points and split on two. The convergence first.

One — kill the 5 kr search. Unanimous. It's a willingness-to-pay anchor that contaminates everything above it. Magnus is correct that this is the strategically critical change in the entire memo, more than tier count.

Two — the 0–20 scale ships, but with two non-negotiable refinements. Lena's point on action-labels (replace "Bevaka" with decision verbs) and Yuki's point on hysteresis (3-point internal swing required before band changes) are both correct and additive. Neither costs engineering time meaningfully. Ship both.

Three — granular debt breakdown is the moat, not the score. The score sells the demo; the breakdown sells the renewal. Yuki's enskild firma carve-out is a real architectural requirement, not paranoia. Build it in from day one.

Now the splits.

Four — tier count. Magnus says four. Founder proposed six. The synthesis: five. Drop Brons (Magnus is right that 299 kr cannibalizes 499 kr without adding a real customer segment). Keep Free as lookup-capped band-only, not full trial — this is the Creditsafe model and it converts. Keep Silver, Guld, Premium, Enterprise. The Premium tier earns its existence specifically because Yuki's regulatory hedge (norric_explain_score_v1) lives there for compliance buyers willing to pay 3-4x for explainability.

Five — sequencing. Astrid wants pilot-first; Henrik wants roadmap-first. Both are right at different time horizons. The pricing page ships now as a sales tool for the Norion close, not as a public commitment. The roadmap decision (which 6 of 8 products go on ice) ships this week, internally, with a written one-pager you send to the investors as a follow-up to the meeting.

Ruling

Ship the repositioning. Five tiers, not six. Action-labels on the score bands. Hysteresis in the model. Granular debt as the Guld+ moat. Pricing page is published as a sales tool, not a public commitment, until first paid contract closes.

Concrete next steps, in order

  1. Write a one-page roadmap memo to investors this week: "Q2 = Kreditvakt close-and-onboard. Sigvik on maintenance. Everything else on ice." This is the most important deliverable in the memo.
  2. Draft the pricing page with five tiers (Free / Silver 499 / Guld 1,499 / Premium 4,999 / Enterprise negotiated). Free is lookup-capped, band-only.
  3. Refactor score display to 0–20 with action-verb Swedish labels. Implement 3-point hysteresis in the band-transition logic. Keep 0–100 in API responses.
  4. Architect debt-breakdown view with enskild firma carve-out before launch. Personal-data fields stripped from Silver; full creditor identity behind Guld + DPA click-through.
  5. Use the new pricing page as the anchor in the Norion / Serafim close conversations next week. Let the first paid contract validate or kill the band structure.
