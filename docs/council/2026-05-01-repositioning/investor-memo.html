The Read

The literal feedback was about pricing tiers and a score scale. The deeper instruction — at least, the one I've taken — is that Kreditvakt's user database is the distribution channel for everything else Norric will ship. Not the brand halo. Not the sales relationship. The database itself.

If Kreditvakt acquires 10,000 verified Swedish business users in the next twelve months, then SIGNAL, Vigil, and Sigvik launch to 10,000 pre-qualified buyers. The customer acquisition cost on products two through six approaches zero.

This reframes the Free tier from a marketing funnel into an asset acquisition channel. Every user query is a signal — about which Swedish companies are being investigated, by whom, and how often. Aggregated, that signal becomes a product Norric can sell back to the market: not "Bolag Y looked at you," which is a legal landmine, but "interest in this sector is up 40% this month," which is a sellable index. This is the institutional-grade analogue to what Klarna and Tink built on consumer data, applied to the B2B credit layer.

The Five Kreditvakt Decisions

  • Score: 0–20 with action labels. The internal model stays at 0–100 (the backtested 6x lift factor depends on it). The displayed score becomes 0–20 in five bands, labelled as decisions — Stabil, Bevaka, Förhöjd risk, Kräv säkerhet, Stoppa krediter — not adjectives. Hysteresis built in so a one-point internal shift does not cause band-hopping.
  • Five tiers, not six. Free (registered, 25 lookups/mo), Silver 499 kr, Guld 1,499 kr, Premium 4,999 kr, Enterprise negotiated. The 5 kr search line is retired entirely — it was a willingness-to-pay anchor that contaminated everything above it.
  • Free tier is a data acquisition channel. Email + organisation number required at signup. BankID gating added in Q3 if signup quality degrades. Every user becomes part of Norric's identity layer, reusable across SIGNAL, Vigil, and Sigvik when they launch.
  • Granular debt breakdown is the moat. Built but gated behind Guld+. Personal-data fields (creditor identity for sole proprietors) carved out at the architecture level — not a policy decision, an architectural one. This is what Creditsafe and UC don't show, and it's what makes the renewal stick.
  • Enterprise pricing leaves the website. Replaced with "Contact sales". Anchoring effect of negotiated pricing only works if it isn't pre-anchored on a public page.

The Risk I'm Watching

The bet underneath this memo is that Norric becomes valuable through accumulation — sensor by sensor, user by user, query by query — and that the Kreditvakt user database is the substrate the rest of the portfolio compounds on top of. The risk is that I am wrong about which product earns the most expensive seat at the table, and I am betting the quarter on Kreditvakt being it. If by end of Q2 we have not closed at least three paying contracts and crossed 1,000 verified Free users, the thesis needs revisiting at the start of Q3.
